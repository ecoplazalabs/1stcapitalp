## What happened

Private credit products are increasingly being marketed to retail investors through cold calling campaigns, including dentists and other professionals outside traditional finance. This retail outreach suggests the private credit sector may be approaching saturation, with promotional activity intensifying as institutional demand moderates.

## Key figures

No specific financial metrics were provided in the source material.

## Why it matters for the lower middle-market

For lower middle-market operators and sponsors, this retail saturation signal carries strategic implications. As private credit providers broaden distribution to unsophisticated investors, competitive pressure may intensify for access to the institutional capital pools that have traditionally underwritten lower middle-market acquisitions and recapitalisations. Additionally, the shift towards retail channels may signal changing risk appetites among traditional institutional providers, potentially affecting pricing and availability of cross-border financing options. The apparent peak in product marketing could presage a correction in private credit terms, favourable to borrowers but potentially disruptive to refinancing timelines.
