## What happened

Spirit Airlines has initiated an orderly wind-down following the collapse of bailout negotiations. Talks with bondholders aimed at securing a government support package did not result in a deal this week.

## Key figures

No specific financial figures, rates or timeframes were disclosed in the source material regarding the bailout discussions or the wind-down process.

## Why it matters for the lower middle-market

The failure of major corporate financing negotiations signals broader credit market tightening that reverberates beyond aviation. Lower middle-market operators should monitor bondholder sentiment and the ease of accessing distressed capital, as deteriorating conditions for larger corporates often precede constraints on acquisition financing and refinancing availability for smaller businesses. Cross-border sponsors evaluating portfolio company resilience may wish to stress-test assumptions around government support and capital market access during extended downturns.
