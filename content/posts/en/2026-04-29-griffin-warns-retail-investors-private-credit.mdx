## What happened

Ken Griffin, founder of Citadel, has flagged concerns that retail investors may not fully understand the liquidity limitations of private credit funds. According to Griffin, some investors may not have recognised they cannot quickly withdraw all their capital from these vehicles.

## Key figures

No specific numerical data was disclosed in the source material regarding fund sizes, investor allocations or redemption timelines.

## Why it matters for the lower middle-market

As private credit has become an increasingly accessible asset class, these observations carry implications for sponsor-backed LBOs and growth financing in the lower middle-market. Retail investor capital inflows into private credit have expanded available liquidity for direct lending, but Griffin's remarks suggest regulatory and educational gaps persist. For LMM operators and PE sponsors executing cross-border transactions, understanding the composition and durability of lender capital remains critical to deployment certainty and refinancing assumptions.

> "Some investors may not have realised they cannot quickly withdraw all their money from funds," noted Griffin's concerns on retail participation.
