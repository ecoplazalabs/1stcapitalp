## What happened

China has blocked Meta's proposed acquisition of Manus, according to CNBC World. The decision represents a significant intervention in cross-border technology deals and reflects Beijing's concerns over the transfer of data, talent and intellectual property to foreign entities during an escalating AI competition with the United States.

## Key figures

- **Deal status**: Blocked by Chinese authorities
- **Acquirer**: Meta
- **Target**: Manus
- **Strategic context**: US-China AI technology rivalry

## Why it matters for the lower middle-market

For lower middle-market operators and their PE sponsors with AI, software or technology assets, this decision signals tightening regulatory scrutiny on cross-border M&A involving China. Companies with significant Chinese operations, data infrastructure or talent bases may face similar intervention. The precedent suggests that regulatory approval timelines for tech transactions could lengthen materially, and deal structures involving data relocation or talent migration warrant closer scrutiny from both transaction advisors and target management teams.

> "Beijing's decision to block Meta's acquisition of Manus is a warning to other startups thinking of relocating data, talent and intellectual property abroad." — CNBC World
