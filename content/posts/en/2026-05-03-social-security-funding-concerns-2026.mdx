## What happened

Policymakers in Washington are debating solutions to address emerging funding challenges within the US Social Security programme. The administration is considering various approaches to address the scheme's projected shortfalls.

## Key figures

- **Timeline**: Funding issues described as imminent
- **Scope**: Multiple policy suggestions under consideration

## Why it matters for the lower middle-market

Social Security's financial trajectory carries downstream consequences for sponsor returns and deal structuring. Sustained pressure on US federal finances influences interest rates, tax policy, and the wider macroeconomic environment within which lower middle-market businesses operate. Private equity sponsors evaluating US-based platforms should monitor fiscal policy developments, as restructuring of entitlement programmes could trigger changes to corporate taxation, labour costs, or the cost of capital. Cross-border acquirers should factor US fiscal sustainability into long-term hold assumptions and exit planning.
