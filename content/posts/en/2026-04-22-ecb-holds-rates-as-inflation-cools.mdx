## What happened

The European Central Bank's Governing Council decided to keep the three key policy rates unchanged, holding the deposit facility rate at 2.50%. The decision matched consensus expectations from a Reuters poll of 62 economists.

## Key figures from the official release

- **Deposit rate:** 2.50% (unchanged)
- **Main refinancing rate:** 2.65% (unchanged)
- **Headline HICP inflation:** 2.1% year-on-year (March)
- **Core inflation:** 2.4% year-on-year (March)

## Why it matters for the lower middle-market

For European companies in the $10M–$100M revenue range, stable rates mean refinancing conditions remain predictable — a welcome reprieve after the 2022–2024 hiking cycle. Sponsors evaluating mezzanine structures and minority equity injections now face a clearer cost-of-capital curve, which tends to accelerate pipeline decisions.

Credit spreads on European high-yield have tightened by roughly 35 basis points since January, per ICE BofA indices, reflecting the same confidence signal.

## What to watch next

The next Governing Council meeting is scheduled for June 2026. Markets are pricing one additional 25bp cut by year-end, though any upside surprise in services inflation could push that decision into 2027.

> "The Governing Council is determined to ensure that inflation returns to our 2% medium-term target in a timely manner."
> — *ECB Monetary Policy Statement, 22 April 2026*
