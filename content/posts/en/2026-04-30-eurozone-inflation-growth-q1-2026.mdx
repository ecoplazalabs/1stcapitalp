## What happened

Eurozone inflation rose to 3% in April, coinciding with data showing first-quarter economic growth of only 0.1%. This combination of elevated price pressures and near-stagnant output presents a challenging macroeconomic backdrop for the currency union's policymakers and corporate operators.

## Key figures

- **Eurozone inflation (April 2026)**: 3%
- **Eurozone GDP growth (Q1 2026)**: 0.1%

## Why it matters for the lower middle-market

For lower middle-market operators and their sponsors, this dynamic threatens margin compression on two fronts. Rising inflation erodes working capital efficiency and input cost management, whilst anaemic growth reduces pricing power and top-line expansion opportunities. Cross-border M&A participants should anticipate heightened cost-of-capital challenges and more cautious buyer appetite across Eurozone targets, particularly those exposed to consumer discretionary or leveraged-growth strategies.
