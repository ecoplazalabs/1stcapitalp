## Headline numbers

According to data compiled by Refinitiv and cited by Reuters, global middle-market M&A deals (defined as $25M–$500M transaction value) rose to **2,847 transactions** in Q1 2026, up from 2,412 in Q1 2025.

## Where the activity is concentrated

- **Europe (ex-UK):** +24% YoY — DACH region leading, industrial carve-outs dominant
- **UK:** +11% YoY — PE-backed bolt-ons account for the majority
- **GCC (UAE, Saudi Arabia):** +31% YoY — cross-border activity into Europe accelerating
- **North America:** +14% YoY — tech services and healthcare leading

## Multiple expansion

Median EV/EBITDA for closed mid-market deals reached **8.6x** in Q1, compared to 7.9x a year earlier. Quality assets in defensible niches continue to command premiums above 10x.

## What it means for sponsors and management teams

Three practical implications for founders and operators:

1. **Processes are tighter.** Competitive auctions are back — selective exclusivity grants replaced broad banker solicitations in 2024–2025 are now giving way to 8-to-12 party processes.
2. **Capital structure flexibility is rewarded.** Mezzanine and unitranche providers are pricing more aggressively as banks rebuild risk appetite.
3. **Cross-border interest is real.** Gulf-based family offices and sovereign-backed vehicles are increasingly active in European mid-market targets.

> "We are seeing the most competitive environment for quality mid-market assets since 2021."
> — *Senior Managing Director, quoted in Reuters*

## The 1st Capital Partners angle

This rebound aligns with our core thesis: lower middle-market companies with $10M–$100M in revenue and $2M–$10M EBITDA remain structurally underserved by bulge-bracket capital. When cycles turn, flexibility in deal structure — mezzanine, minority equity, or hybrid — wins over pure leverage plays.
